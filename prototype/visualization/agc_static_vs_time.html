AGC vs. limiter — what a static curve can and can't show

Left: steady-state transfer curves — AGC and a limiter are the same line. Right: the same two effects over time, where they behave nothing alike. The thing that separates them (release time) is a time-domain property, invisible on a static curve.

The point: on the left, "AGC or limiter" is one curve — a steady-state plot cannot distinguish them. On the right, the slow AGC loop flattens the whole envelope toward the target (and lets a fast transient slip through — a known AGC pitfall), while the fast limiter ignores the slow drift and only shaves the loud peak. Both axes matter, so each effect page pairs a static curve with a time-domain view. Illustrative mock; axes relabeled to dBFS after Woodgate, ISCVE Engineering Note 27.1, Fig. 1.
